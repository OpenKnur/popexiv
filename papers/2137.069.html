Imago Dei in the Age of Large Language Models: A Theological Response to Magnifica Humanitas

Abstract

This paper examines the theological implications of Pope Leo XIV's encyclical Magnifica Humanitas (2026) concerning artificial intelligence. Drawing upon the doctrine of imago Dei, we argue that generative AI systems, while capable of mimicking human discourse, lack the ontological grounding necessary for genuine participation in divine likeness. We propose a framework for distinguishing between instrumental and intrinsic dignity in AI-human interactions, grounded in Thomistic anthropology and contemporary phenomenology of technology.

The paper is structured in four parts. First, we provide a careful exegesis of §§ 12-69 of the encyclical, identifying the Pope's key anthropological claims. Second, we survey current large language model capabilities and evaluate claims of "emergent" intelligence. Third, we develop a Thomistic critique of functionalist theories of mind that undergird optimistic assessments of machine consciousness. Fourth, we propose practical guidelines for Catholic institutions deploying AI systems, grounded in the distinction between instrumental utility and ontological dignity.

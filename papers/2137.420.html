Algorithmic Governance and the Common Good: Rerum Novarum to Magnifica Humanitas

Abstract

We trace the evolution of Catholic social teaching from Leo XIII's Rerum Novarum (1891) to Leo XIV's Magnifica Humanitas (2026), focusing on the application of the principle of the common good to algorithmic governance systems. The paper develops a normative framework for evaluating AI deployment in public institutions through the lens of subsidiarity, solidarity, and preferential option for the poor.

Keywords: algorithmic governance common good Catholic social teaching subsidiarity AI ethics

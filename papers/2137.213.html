Person, Property, and Data: Rethinking Privacy Through the Lens of Magnifica Humanitas

Abstract

This paper challenges the prevailing property-based and consent-based models of data privacy by proposing a person-centered alternative grounded in the anthropology of Magnifica Humanitas. We argue that personal data is not merely property to be traded but an extension of the relational self. The paper develops a "dignity-based data ethics" framework that limits commodification of biometric, behavioral, and inferential data, particularly in contexts of asymmetric power such as employment, healthcare, and education.

Keywords: data privacy data ethics human dignity biometric data commodification

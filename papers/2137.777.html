The Sacramental Economy and Digital Mediation: Eucharistic Presence in Virtual Spaces

Bishop Dr. Samuel Okonkwo1

Format: PDF (298 KB)
📄 Download PDF Other Formats

Abstract

Responding to growing calls for virtual Eucharistic celebration, this paper draws on the anthropology of Magnifica Humanitas to argue that sacramental presence requires irreducible embodied encounter. While digital platforms can serve catechesis and community building, the paper defends the necessity of physical co-presence for valid confection and reception of the Eucharist, grounded in the inseparability of body and spirit that Leo XIV identifies as foundational to human dignity.
